<p><ul><li>Infrastructure</li><li>Ad-Hoc</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Wireless Mesh Networking Devices encompass two primary types: Infrastructure and Ad-Hoc networks. </p><p>Infrastructure networks utilize fixed nodes that are strategically deployed to create a robust, scalable mesh where devices communicate through established routes, ideal for larger, organized environments. In contrast, Ad-Hoc networks are temporary and spontaneous, formed dynamically by devices connecting directly to each other without a central management system, making them suitable for mobile or emergency situations where quick setup is essential. Both types enhance connectivity and reliability in varying scenarios.</p></p>
