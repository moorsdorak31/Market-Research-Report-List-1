<p><p>The hatchback aluminum alloy wheel market is primarily categorized into two types: casting and forging. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds to create wheels, allowing for complex designs and lower production costs. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping aluminum under high pressure, resulting in stronger and lighter wheels with enhanced performance characteristics. Both methods cater to different consumer preferences, with casting offering aesthetic versatility and forging providing superior durability and structural integrity for hatchback vehicles.</p></p>
