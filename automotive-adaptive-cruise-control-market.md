<p><p>The automotive adaptive cruise control market is categorized into two main types: Normal Adaptive Cruise Control Systems and Connected Adaptive Cruise Control Systems. </p><p>Normal Adaptive Cruise Control adjusts a vehicle’s speed based on the distance to the car ahead, providing a safer driving experience without driver intervention. Connected Adaptive Cruise Control, on the other hand, relies on vehicle-to-vehicle and vehicle-to-infrastructure communication, allowing for real-time traffic updates and enhanced coordination between vehicles, leading to improved efficiency and safety on the roads.</p></p>
